    • The hobbits

    "The Hobbit" is a fantasy novel written by J.R.R. Tolkien. It follows the adventures of Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit who is unexpectedly recruited by the wizard Gandalf to join a group of dwarves on a quest to reclaim their homeland from the fearsome dragon Smaug. Bilbo faces numerous challenges and encounters magical creatures like trolls, goblins, and a mysterious creature named Gollum. With the help of his newfound courage and the magical ring he discovers, Bilbo plays a crucial role in the success of the mission. Along the way, he undergoes personal growth and develops lasting friendships, ultimately returning home as a changed hobbit.

    The adventure of Sherlock Holmes

    "The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes" is a collection of twelve short stories written by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. The stories feature the renowned detective Sherlock Holmes and his loyal companion Dr. John Watson as they solve various intriguing mysteries in late 19th-century London. From deciphering coded messages to unraveling baffling crimes, Holmes employs his sharp intellect, deductive reasoning, and keen observational skills to crack the cases. Each story showcases Holmes' brilliance and showcases his iconic methods of investigation, making "The Adventures of Sherlock Holmes" a classic work in the detective genre.

    Gitanjali

    "Gitanjali" is a collection of poems written by the renowned Indian poet Rabindranath Tagore. Originally published in 1910, it explores themes of love, spirituality, and the human connection to nature. The collection showcases Tagore's lyrical and contemplative style, earning him the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913 and establishing him as a prominent figure in world literature."Gitanjali" garnered international acclaim, propelling Tagore to become the first non-European Nobel laureate in Literature in 1913. The collection continues to be celebrated as a significant work in world literature, showcasing Tagore's ability to capture the essence of human experience in poetic form.

    Hamlet

    "Hamlet" by William Shakespeare is a tragic play that follows the story of Prince Hamlet of Denmark, who seeks revenge for his father's murder. After his father's ghost reveals that he was killed by his own brother, Claudius, who then married Hamlet's mother, Gertrude, Hamlet becomes consumed by thoughts of vengeance. Throughout the play, he feigns madness, contemplates the nature of life and death, and struggles with his own indecisiveness. The story is filled with betrayal, deceit, and tragedy, ultimately culminating in a dramatic final scene where almost all the characters meet their demise.

    The Jungle Book

    "The Jungle Book" is a classic children's novel written by Rudyard Kipling. It tells the story of Mowgli, a young boy who is raised by a pack of wolves in the Indian jungle after being separated from his human parents. Mowgli's adventures in the jungle involve encounters with various animals, including Baloo the bear, Bagheera the panther, and the fearsome tiger, Shere Khan. With the guidance of his animal friends, Mowgli learns valuable life lessons about survival, friendship, and finding his place in the world.
